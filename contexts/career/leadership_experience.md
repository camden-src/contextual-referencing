## PROBLEM-SOLVING & DECISION-MAKING

### Systems Thinking

- **Balance urgency with long-term thinking:** Solve immediate problems while building infrastructure that stands the test of time
- **Cost optimization:** Design solutions that balance performance, security, and cost-efficiency
- **Reliability focus:** Build systems that protect uptime and enable rapid incident response
- **Scalability planning:** Architect infrastructure that scales with organizational growth

### Technical Decision-Making

- **Infrastructure choices:** Evaluate when containerization, serverless, or traditional compute models best serve specific use cases
- **Tool selection:** Choose tools based on team needs, not hype (Terraform over alternatives, for example)
- **Compliance balance:** Meet security requirements while maintaining agility for product teams
- **Automation priorities:** Identify highest-impact automation to reduce operational toil

### Incident Response

- **Proactive approach:** Build monitoring that detects issues before they impact users
- **Collaborative troubleshooting:** Work with teams to diagnose and resolve production issues
- **Post-mortems:** Learn from incidents to improve systems and processes
- **Communication:** Clear status updates during incidents

## CULTURAL VALUES & TEAM DYNAMICS

### Team Culture Preferences

- **Collaborative problem-solving:** Value teams that work together to solve complex challenges
- **Psychological safety:** Environments where it's safe to raise concerns and make mistakes
- **Work-life balance:** Sustainable pace, respect for personal time
- **Inclusive:** Value diverse perspectives and backgrounds

### Red Flags (Cultures to Avoid)

- **"Elite team not family" Navy SEALs analogies** - High-pressure, unsustainable cultures (e.g., TetraScience)
- **Radical transparency without compassion** - Harsh feedback cultures
- **Revenue obsession over team well-being** - Burnout-inducing environments
- **Lack of work-life balance** - "Always on" expectations

## PREFERRED ROLE TYPES

### Individual Contributor (IC) Roles

**Comfortable as senior IC when:**
- Role offers ownership over infrastructure decisions
- Opportunity to mentor and influence without formal management
- Technical depth valued over people management
- Autonomy to shape infrastructure strategy

### Technical Leadership Roles

**Comfortable as tech lead/manager when:**
- Small to medium team size (5-10 engineers)
- Hands-on technical work remains significant portion of role
- Culture values technical depth in leadership
- Collaborative, not command-and-control management style

### Staff/Principal Engineer

**Ideal for:**
- System-wide architectural decisions
- Setting technical direction and standards
- Mentoring across multiple teams
- Influencing engineering culture and practices
